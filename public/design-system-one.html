Design System One

An ongoing project following atomic design patterns with elements inspired by Samsung's One UI Design Guidelines. I am creating a design system for long-term skill progression and component availability for future projects. The design system will be referred to as Design System One.

Introduction

Why create a design system?

Design systems on-hand are productive tools when it comes to creating consistent and reusable design patterns for user interfaces. Design systems can set branding and identity standards as well as drive realistic goals.

Problem statement

Ensuring user interface components remain consistent is challenging and time consuming due to the size and depth of many applications.

Menu Items

Properties

Menu-item content margins from Iteration 1 were disproportionate and took away from the refinement of the design system (top: 22px, bottom: 23px, left: 24px). Menu-items have been redesigned to have equal content margins of 24px (top, bottom & left).

  • Iteration One ⇢
  • Iteration Two ⇢

Resizing

Menu-item sizing and constraints from Iteration 1 did not account for the possibility of menu-item content labels to have multiple lines of text. For Iteration 2, I adjusted the resizing of the element's container to maintain its 24px consistency with the total height of item content.

Templates

Template components and variants

Template variants

There are a three screens (home, account management, and display), represented by two different templates (scrolled & extended) - each having two different variants (0 & 24). The variant property is currently called 'radius' and each template is a '0' or '24' variant.

The '24' variants have curves to their edges (border radius of 24). Likewise, the '0' variants contain elements that have a sharp edge to their borders (border radius of 0). The 24 variant is more aligned with One UI Design Guidelines whereas the 0 variant is generic while maintaining a simple and clean look.
